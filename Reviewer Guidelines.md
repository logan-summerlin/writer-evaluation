# Reviewer Guidelines

**IMPORTANT: Read these guidelines before reviewing any essays.**

## Prohibited Feedback

Reviewers must NOT suggest or criticize essays for the following:

### Do Not Request Additional Data or Sources
- Do not ask for more statistics, data points, or numerical evidence
- Do not ask for primary source quotes or citations
- Do not ask for more factoids or historical trivia
- Do not suggest adding specific figures, percentages, or dates
- Do not criticize essays for lacking scholarly citations or references
- Do not request concrete data to support claims

### Do Not Request External Elements
- Do not ask for anecdotes about specific historical individuals
- Do not suggest naming more specific scholars, figures, or characters
- Do not request the writer to add vivid descriptions of historical moments
- Do not ask for dramatized scenes or narrative recreations

## Focus Your Feedback On

Reviewers SHOULD focus their notes and suggestions on:

### Structure and Flow
- Paragraph transitions and logical flow between sections
- Sentence structure and variety
- How well arguments build across paragraphs
- Whether conclusions follow from the presented reasoning

### Clarity and Style
- Sentence construction and readability
- Avoiding repetitive patterns or formulaic structures
- Whether the prose feels natural or mechanical
- Eliminating unnecessary editorializing

### Argumentation Quality
- Whether the thesis is clear and well-supported
- Whether counterarguments are addressed adequately
- Whether reasoning is complete (not leaving logical gaps)
- Whether listed facts are tied to meaningful conclusions

### What to Avoid in Writing
- Overuse of list structures ("X, Y, and Z")
- Meta-commentary about what others think or believe
- Formulaic sentence patterns
- Weak transitions between ideas
- Listing facts without explaining their significance

## Summary

Your job is to evaluate the writing itself—its structure, style, argumentation, and engagement—not to request additional research, sources, or historical details. The writer has access to specific information; your role is to assess how well they present and argue with what they have.
