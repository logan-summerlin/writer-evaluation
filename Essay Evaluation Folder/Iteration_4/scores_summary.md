## Common Reviewer Observations

### Improvements from Iteration 3
- Clearer, bolder thesis statements
- Better use of section headers
- More compelling paradox framing
- Improved argumentation and logical flow
- Active voice usage improved (especially in top-performing essays)

### Persistent Weaknesses
1. **Mechanical prose patterns**: Too many short declarative sentences in sequence
2. **Formulaic transitions**: Section openings feel predictable and workmanlike
3. **Bolded thesis statements**: Disrupts natural essay flow, feels like textbook convention
4. **Lack of distinctive voice**: Competent but anonymous, lacking personality
5. **Some sections feel list-like**: Catalog problems rather than integrated analysis
6. **Summary over analysis**: Some essays summarize events rather than analyzing them

### What Distinguished Top Essays (9, 7, 5)
- Essay 9 (Medici): Tight argumentative thread, sophisticated cultural-political analysis
- Essay 7 (Caesar): Structural failure argument transcends usual narrative, strong opening paradox
- Essay 5 (Terror): Sophisticated psychological analysis, handled complex morality well
